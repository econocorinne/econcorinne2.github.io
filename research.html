Working Papers

"What Drives Occupational Wage Inequality in the U.S.: Productivity or Rent Sharing?"

This paper examines the factors contributing to occupational wage inequality in the U.S., focusing on whether productivity differences or rent sharing are the primary drivers.

"Trends in U.S.Wage Inequality: Revising the Revisionists. A Replication Study of Autor, Katz, and Kearney (The Review of Economics and Statistics, 2008)" Journal of Comments and Replications in Economics, Vol 3(2024-4)

This study revisits the findings of Autor, Katz, and Kearney, exploring trends in U.S. wage inequality and replicating their analysis to assess the robustness of their results.

Work in Progress

"Aging Workforces and the Decline in the Large Firm Wage Premium"

This paper investigates the impact of aging workforces on the decline in the large firm wage premium, focusing on firm-level dynamics and workforce composition.

"The Task Approach to Labor Markets in the Presence of Search Frictions" with Nils Lehr

This work develops a task-based approach to labor markets under search frictions, analyzing how task heterogeneity and frictions affect employment outcomes.

"Gender Bias in the Labor Market: Evidence from Newspaper Job Ads" with Vikram Dixit

This paper explores gender biases in job ads across multiple industries. It examines how job advertisements have historically been tailored towards one gender and the implications for labor market outcomes. Using newspaper data, we identify subtle patterns in job ads that suggest gender preferences in hiring.
